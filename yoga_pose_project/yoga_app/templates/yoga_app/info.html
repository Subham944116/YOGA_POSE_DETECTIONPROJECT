What is Yoga?

Yoga is both a philosophy and a physical practice originating in ancient India. It encompasses ethical living, breath control, body postures, meditation, and insight — aiming at integrating body, breath, and mind to enhance well-being.

Philosophy

Roots in the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, the Bhagavad Gita, and the Upanishads. Themes: self-discipline (tapas), non-attachment (vairagya), and awareness (dharana).

Practice Elements

Includes yamas & niyamas (ethical rules), asanas (postures), pranayama (breath work), pratyahara (sense withdrawal), dharana (concentration), dhyana (meditation), and samadhi (absorption).

History & Origins

Yoga's origins trace back over 3,000 years in the Indian subcontinent. Early practices were meditative; asanas became more central later. Over centuries yoga evolved into many schools and lineages, both traditional and modern.

Core Practices

  • Asanas (Postures): Align the body to improve strength, balance, and flexibility.
  • Pranayama (Breathwork): Techniques like Ujjayi, Nadi Shodhana (alternate nostril), Kapalabhati to regulate prana (life force).
  • Meditation & Mindfulness: Practices to cultivate concentration and awareness.
  • Yamas & Niyamas: Ethical guidelines for living (e.g., non-violence, truthfulness, contentment).

Benefits of Regular Practice

  • Improved flexibility, strength, and posture
  • Reduced stress, anxiety, and improved mood
  • Better sleep, concentration, and respiratory health
  • Supports joint health and injury prevention when practised mindfully

Safety & Precautions

Always listen to your body. Avoid forcing into postures. Consult a healthcare professional for chronic conditions, pregnancy, or recent surgery. Use props (blocks, straps, bolsters) and modify poses to avoid strain.

Tips for Beginners

  1. Warm up gently before deeper stretches.
  2. Practice with intention, not speed.
  3. Use blocks and straps for support.
  4. Focus on breath coordination.
  5. Keep a practice journal to track progress.
